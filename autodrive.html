F1-Tenth Autonomous Vehicle Research

Overview

In the rapidly advancing field of autonomous vehicles, my role in the F1-Tenth project focused on pushing the boundaries of scaled-down self-driving technology. As part of a multidisciplinary team, I spearheaded the creation of an experimental testbed that allowed us to transition from theoretical research into real-world applications. From implementing custom control algorithms to ensuring that simulation-based designs translated effectively to physical hardware, this project was at the forefront of autonomous systems research.

Role: Autonomous Vehicle Research Engineer

Duration: July 2023 — May 2024

View Research Poster

Problem

The Challenge

Autonomous driving continues to be one of the most significant challenges in transportation technology. Our research team set out to develop a flexible and scalable testbed, aimed at advancing self-driving capabilities while addressing the complex challenges of transitioning algorithms from simulation to real-world environments. The challenge was not just building an autonomous vehicle but ensuring that it could handle real-world driving scenarios while improving on existing reinforcement learning models.

Solution

Our Approach

The F1-Tenth platform provided a flexible foundation for developing advanced control strategies. My work centered on designing custom controllers for tasks like driving straight and implementing a PID-based wall-following system, enabling the vehicle to navigate autonomously. I focused on sim-to-real adjustments to align simulation with real-world performance, solving issues like steering instability by dynamically modifying the steering angle to correct oscillations.

I fine-tuned the PID control parameters to improve wall-following precision, ensuring smooth navigation. Additionally, I used VESC software to enhance motor control, achieving refined acceleration. Setting up and calibrating a depth perception camera enabled the vehicle to capture crucial environmental data and supported real-time monitoring through live video streaming. These efforts advanced the vehicle's performance and reliability in both simulation and real-world testing.

Impact

The Results

The impact of this project extended beyond simply getting an autonomous vehicle to function. By creating a comprehensive testbed, we were able to advance both the research and practical aspects of self-driving systems. The custom control algorithms we developed played a key role in optimizing the vehicle's performance, while the integration of hardware and software allowed for smoother, more reliable real-world driving. Additionally, the experience I gained in fine-tuning algorithms, real-time simulation, and sensor calibration contributed to the growing body of knowledge in autonomous vehicle research.

Takeaways

Lessons Learned

Throughout this project, I honed my skills in robotics software, particularly with ROS and RViz, and gained a deep understanding of how reinforcement learning and control systems can be applied to real-world problems. One of the most valuable lessons was the importance of sim-to-real consistency—bridging the gap between simulations and physical systems is critical for the success of autonomous systems in real-world scenarios. The hands-on work with the F1-Tenth car has been pivotal in my development as an engineer and researcher, and has solidified my passion for working at the intersection of hardware, software, and machine learning.
